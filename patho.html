PATHOLOGY MCQ SURVIVAL SHEET

Golden buzzwords only. Read once, retain forever.

1. RESPIRATORY SYSTEM ▼
Obstructive Lung Diseases — KEY RULE
All obstructive = decreased FEV1/FVC ratio. Distinguish by cause/mechanism.
Asthma
Buzzword: Reversible bronchoconstriction, Curschmann spirals, Charcot-Leyden crystals
Cells: Eosinophils — the signature cell
Mechanism: Type I hypersensitivity (IgE-mediated)
Mucus plugging → Curschmann spirals in sputum
Chronic Bronchitis
Definition: Productive cough ≥3 months/year for ≥2 consecutive years
Buzzword: Blue bloater, hypercapnia, cyanosis, edema
Pathology: Reid index >50% (mucus gland hypertrophy)
Cause: Smoking → goblet cell hyperplasia
Emphysema
Buzzword: Pink puffer, barrel chest, pursed lips
Mechanism: Protease-antiprotease imbalance → alveolar wall destruction
Centriacinar: Smoking (upper lobes affected)
Panacinar: Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency (lower lobes)
Bronchiectasis
Buzzword: Permanent dilation of bronchi, copious foul-smelling sputum
Cause: Cystic fibrosis (most common), Kartagener syndrome
Kartagener: Bronchiectasis + situs inversus + infertility
Pneumonia
Lobar pneumonia stages: Congestion → Red hepatization → Grey hepatization → Resolution
Most common cause lobar: Streptococcus pneumoniae
Atypical pneumonia: Mycoplasma — "walking pneumonia", cold agglutinins
Bronchopneumonia: Patchy, bilateral, Staphylococcus/H. influenzae
ARDS
Buzzword: Hyaline membrane formation, diffuse alveolar damage
Mechanism: Increased capillary permeability → non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema
Key: Normal PCWP (rules out cardiac cause)
Pleural Effusion
Transudate: Heart failure, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome (low protein)
Exudate: Infection, malignancy, inflammation (high protein)
Light's criteria: Exudate if protein >0.5 or LDH >0.6 of serum
Lung Tumors
Most common primary: Adenocarcinoma (peripheral, non-smokers possible)
Central location: Squamous cell + Small cell
Squamous cell: Keratin pearls, cavitation, PTHrP (hypercalcemia)
Small cell: ACTH/ADH/Eaton-Lambert, worst prognosis, neuroendocrine
Pancoast tumor: Apex → Horner syndrome (ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis)
2. CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM ▼
Atherosclerosis
First lesion: Fatty streak (reversible)
Key cell: Foam cell = macrophage engulfing LDL
Plaque: Fibrous cap + necrotic core + calcium
Most dangerous: Plaque rupture → thrombus
Ischemic Heart Disease
0–24 hrs: Coagulative necrosis, wavy fibers, no neutrophils yet
1–3 days: Neutrophil infiltration (yellow softening)
3–10 days: Macrophages (risk of rupture!)
Weeks: Granulation tissue → scar
Earliest marker: Troponin I/T (most specific)
STEMI: Full thickness = transmural. NSTEMI = subendocardial
Heart Failure
Left HF: Pulmonary edema, orthopnea, PND, hemosiderin-laden macrophages ("heart failure cells")
Right HF: Nutmeg liver, JVD, peripheral edema, ascites
Right HF most commonly caused by Left HF
Hypertensive Heart Disease
Buzzword: Concentric LV hypertrophy (pressure overload)
Dilated cardiomyopathy: Volume overload → eccentric hypertrophy
Infective Endocarditis
Acute: S. aureus — destroys normal valves, large friable vegetations
Subacute: Strep viridans — damaged valves (dental procedures)
Osler nodes: Painful (fingers/toes)
Janeway lesions: Painless (palms/soles)
Roth spots: Retinal hemorrhages with white centers
Rheumatic Heart Disease
Cause: Group A Strep → molecular mimicry
Buzzword: Aschoff bodies (pathognomonic) + Anitschkow cells (caterpillar cells)
Most common valve: Mitral stenosis
Vegetations: Small, along line of closure (sterile)
3. THYROID GLAND ▼
Nodular Goiter
Cause: Iodine deficiency (most common worldwide)
Buzzword: Colloid-filled follicles, multinodular
Key: Euthyroid usually
Thyroiditis Types
Hashimoto: Anti-TPO, anti-thyroglobulin, Hürthle cells, most common hypothyroid cause
De Quervain (Granulomatous): Post-viral, painful, giant cells, transient hyperthyroid → hypothyroid
Riedel: Stone-hard fibrosis, can mimic cancer
Graves disease: TSH receptor antibodies, exophthalmos, pretibial myxedema
4. GIT ▼
Oral Cavity Quick Hits
Aphthous ulcer: Painful, NOT HSV, no vesicles, stress-related
HSV: Vesicles that rupture → ulcers
Leukoplakia: White patch, can't wipe off, premalignant
Oral candidiasis: White patch, CAN wipe off, pseudohyphae
Pyogenic granuloma: Lobular capillary hemangioma, bleeds easily, pregnancy
Mucocele: Lower lip, mucin spillage from minor salivary gland
Esophagus
Barrett esophagus: Squamous → Intestinal metaplasia (goblet cells), GERD complication, risk of adenocarcinoma
Achalasia: Failure of LES to relax, loss of Auerbach plexus, bird-beak on barium
Mallory-Weiss: Longitudinal tear, alcoholic vomiting
Varices: Portal hypertension → dilated submucosal veins, massive bleed
Stomach
H. pylori: Most common cause of peptic ulcer + gastric carcinoma
Gastric ulcer: Pain with eating (vs. duodenal = relief with eating)
Gastric carcinoma: Signet ring cells (abundant mucin pushes nucleus aside)
Krukenberg tumor: Gastric carcinoma metastasis to ovaries
Virchow node: Left supraclavicular node → gastric/GI malignancy
IBD — Crohn's vs Ulcerative Colitis
Crohn's: Any part mouth-to-anus, skip lesions, transmural, cobblestone, fistulas, granulomas, NO blood typically
UC: Rectum upward, continuous, mucosal only, crypt abscesses, bloody diarrhea
Cancer risk: UC > Crohn's
Crohn = Cobblestone, Creeping fat, skip lesions | UC = Uniform, Ulcers, rectum Up
Appendicitis
Most common cause: Fecalith (adults), lymphoid hyperplasia (children)
Cells: Neutrophil infiltration of muscularis
Liver
Hep A: Fecal-oral, NO chronicity, anti-HAV IgM
Hep B: HBsAg = active infection, HBsAb = immunity, HBeAg = high infectivity
Hep C: Most common chronic hepatitis, blood-borne, cirrhosis + HCC
Fatty liver: Alcoholic = centrilobular; NAFLD = metabolic syndrome
Jaundice types: Pre-hepatic = unconjugated ↑ | Hepatic = both | Post-hepatic = conjugated ↑ (dark urine, pale stool)
Wilson's: Copper accumulation, Kayser-Fleischer rings, ceruloplasmin ↓
Hemochromatosis: Iron, "bronze diabetes," HFE gene
5. RENAL SYSTEM ▼
Nephrotic vs Nephritic — MASTER THIS
Nephrotic = protein loss (>3.5g/day) → edema, hypoalbuminemia, hyperlipidemia, lipiduria. NO hematuria.
Nephritic = inflammation → hematuria, RBC casts, hypertension, oliguria, mild proteinuria.
Nephrotic Syndromes
Children #1: Minimal Change Disease — effacement of podocyte foot processes (EM), steroid-responsive
Adults #1: Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) — HIV, heroin
Membranous: "Spike and dome," hepatitis B, SLE, thickened BM
Diabetic: Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules
Nephritic Syndromes
Post-strep GN: "Humps" on EM (subepithelial deposits), low C3, hematuria in child after sore throat
IgA nephropathy (Berger): Hematuria during/after URI (vs. post-strep = 2–3 weeks after)
Goodpasture: Linear IgG deposits, anti-GBM antibody, lungs + kidneys
Lupus nephritis: "Full house" immunofluorescence (IgG+IgM+IgA+C3+C1q)
Renal Tumors
Renal Cell Carcinoma: Clear cells (lipid-rich), VHL gene, classic triad: flank pain + hematuria + mass
